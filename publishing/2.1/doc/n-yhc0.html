<body>

Body of the Article

Definition

The main textual portion of the article that conveys the content

Related Elements

A journal article <article> may be divided into four (more typically, three) parts:

  1. the <front> (the article metadata or header information, which contains both journal and article metadata);
  2. the <body> (textual and graphical content of the article);
  3. any <back> (ancillary information such as a glossary, reference list, or appendix); and
  4. either a series of <response> elements (A response is a commentary on the article itself.) or a series of <sub-article> elements (Sub-articles are smaller articles completely contained within the article.).

Model Information

Content Model

<!ELEMENT  body         %body-model;                                 >

Description

The following, in order:

  • Any combination of:
    • Array (Simple Tabular Array) <array>
    • Boxed Text <boxed-text>
    • Chemical Structure (Display) <chem-struct>
    • Chemical Structure Wrapper <chem-struct-wrapper>
    • Figure <fig>
    • Figure Group <fig-group>
    • Graphic <graphic>
    • Media Object <media>
    • Preformatted Text <preformat>
    • Supplementary Material <supplementary-material>
    • Table Wrapper <table-wrap>
    • Table Wrapper Group <table-wrap-group>
    • Formula, Display <disp-formula>
    • Paragraph <p>
    • Definition List <def-list>
    • List <list>
    • Tex Math Equation <tex-math>
    • Math (MathML 2.0 DTD) <mml:math>
    • Related Article Information <related-article>
    • Quote, Displayed <disp-quote>
    • Speech <speech>
    • Statement, Formal <statement>
    • Verse Form for Poetry <verse-group>
  • Section <sec>, zero or more

This element may be contained in:

<article> Article; <response> Response; <sub-article> Sub-Article
