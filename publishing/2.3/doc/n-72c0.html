<article-meta>

Article Metadata

Definition

Metadata is (loosely) “data about the data”. The metadata for an article is information concerning the article, for example, bibliographic data such as authorship, article title, copyright year, and publication date; descriptive material such as keywords and abstracts; or any article identifying numbers. The article metadata is not part of the body text or graphics of the article proper, but serves to identify or describe the article.

Remarks

Some journal DTDs identify three types of metadata: that concerning the specific article, that concerning the journal, and that concerning the issue of the journal that contains the article. In this DTD, the metadata for the issue and the article has been placed together inside the <article-meta> and the journal metadata into the <journal-meta> element.

Related Elements

Older, MAJOUR-style journal DTDs split the metadata in a journal article into three categories: journal metadata, article metadata, and issue metadata. In this Suite, the metadata specific to the journal is within the element <journal-meta> and the metadata specific both to the article and to the particular issue of the journal (if any) is within the <article-meta> element.

Model Information

Content Model

<!ELEMENT  article-meta %article-meta-model;                         >

Description

The following, in order:

  • Article Identifier <article-id>, zero or more
  • Article Grouping Data <article-categories>, zero or one
  • Title Group <title-group>
  • Any combination of:
    • Contributor Group <contrib-group>
    • Affiliation <aff>
  • Author Note Group <author-notes>, zero or one
  • Publication Date <pub-date>, one or more
  • Volume Number <volume>, zero or one
  • Volume Identifier <volume-id>, zero or more
  • Issue Number <issue>, zero or one
  • Issue Identifier <issue-id>, zero or more
  • Issue Title <issue-title>, zero or more
  • Supplement Information <supplement>, zero or one
  • Optionally any one of:
    • The following, in order:
      • First Page <fpage>
      • Last Page <lpage>, zero or one
      • Page Ranges <page-range>, zero or one
    • Electronic Location Identifier <elocation-id>
  • Any combination of:
    • All the address linking elements:
      • Email Address <email>
      • External Link <ext-link>
      • Uniform Resource Indicator (URI) <uri>
    • Product Information <product>
    • Supplementary Material <supplementary-material>
  • History: Document History <history>, zero or one
  • Copyright Statement <copyright-statement>, zero or one
  • Copyright Year <copyright-year>, zero or one
  • License Information <license>, zero or one
  • Permissions <permissions>, zero or one
  • URI for This Same Article Online <self-uri>, zero or more
  • Related Article Information <related-article>, zero or more
  • Abstract <abstract>, zero or more
  • Translated Abstract <trans-abstract>, zero or more
  • Keyword Group <kwd-group>, zero or more
  • Contract Number <contract-num>, zero or more
  • Contract Sponsor <contract-sponsor>, zero or more
  • Grant Number <grant-num>, zero or more
  • Grant Sponsor <grant-sponsor>, zero or more
  • Conference Information <conference>, zero or more
  • Counts <counts>, zero or one
  • Custom Metadata Wrapper <custom-meta-wrap>, zero or more
